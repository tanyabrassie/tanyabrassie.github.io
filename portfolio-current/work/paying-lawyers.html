Lawpay.com Re-Design
Role: User Research & Information Architecture

Overview

Lawpay is payment processing tool for lawyers. In short, it routes credit card transactions to their oppropriate accounts so lawyers don't have to worry about the co-mingling of fees. While the tool itself is useful, its website wasn't and as a UX Intern at Springbox, it was my job to revamp the site architecture.

Problems with the existing site architecture included:

  • Tons of redundant content
  • Unclear calls to action
  • Vague navigational structure and unclear user paths
  • No feedback (i.e. active states) for the user to see where he/she is
  • Inconsistent terminology

Below we have a side by side comparison of the home page and feature page demonstrating some of the issues mentioned above. The image on the left is the homepage while the image on the right is the features landing page. If you look at the global navigation, you'll notice its appearance never changes, making it difficult to know which page you're on. (2) Further compounding this confusion is the repetitive content: the hero image from the homepage is repeated on the content page and images such as the graph screenshot are used across site sections and content blocks. (1 & 3)

A look at the site map below further illstrates the issue. The site suffered from a shallow but wide structure meaning that many pages were only reachable through one path or existed as dead ends. Additionally, many pages included slightly similar content with slightly different wording descriptions which added even more confusion. Had I just looked at this page? Is this page describing the same thing or another product?
