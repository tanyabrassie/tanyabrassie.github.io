PCLN Extras
Role: Designer

Overview

Below are some print works I've made for the Priceline office. Since my job is limited to creating things in the digital realm, being able to see a design come to life in the physical world provides a unique sense of satiscation. I also believe that offices have become too digitally focused--most communication whether it be person to person or company wide takes place via email. And through this noise its hard to get a message across, so to encourage participation in our monthly GUTs speaker series, I decided to design and hang posters around the office. So far, it's seeemd to work; GUTS attendance has increased and requests to present content for future series have also gone up.

Another area which could greatly benefit from some print work is our intern program. Currently, we have around 30 interns each summer. Interns are given generic onboarding materials in addition to a lot of hyperlinks going to various sources. From my days as an intern, I can recall it was difficult to know where to find the inforamtion I needed. I decided some sort of guide was needed. Below is an idea I have for an intern package. The package would be travel themed and contain a luggage tag, intern guidebook, internary and boarding pass (containing perhaps tickets to exchange for various goodies). This would be given to interns on their first day in a bag resemebling a suitcase. Since this is a side-project and something I've only been working on in my free time, only portions of the package have been completed.
