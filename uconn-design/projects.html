About Me Project (100 pts) – Due Sept. 17th

To get acquainted with one another, please write 5 short phrases about yourself. These can be things you like, a mention of where you grew up, something you do or don't believe, a phobia, something no one else knows about you, etc. Be creative!

We are going to use these phrases to begin to explore HTML, and to build our first webpages together!

You can see 5 phrases I wrote about myself here. This page was written in HTML. HTML is used to identify and display content, not to style it, that's why it looks so boring.

Your final About Me page will have at minimum a photo, 5 short phrases about you, a paragraph and a link. All of these should be styled with CSS located in a separate style sheet. If you want to add more, feel free! We'll look at examples of About Me pages in class for inspiration.

To complete the project, push the finished code to your github and post the link to your About Me page to our slack chat in the "aboutmepage" channel.

An example finished About Me page can be seen here.

Project Requirements
  1. The following basic HTML tags used appropriately: <!DOCTYPE html>, <html>, <head>, <span>, <div>, <title>, <body>, <p>, <ol>, or <ul>, <li>, <a>, <img>.
  2. An external file sheet called labeled "style.css" containing your CSS. Note: do not include any inline HTML styles.
  3. Styles applied the content within each of the mandatory tags mentioned above. Styling related to the concept of the Box Model (e.g. padding, margin and border) around any element of your choice.
  4. Class names on at least three tags of your choosing, and CSS associated with those tags in your style.css file.
  5. Use of any webfont from Google Fonts.
Make sure your code is saved in a folder called "portfolio" and pushed to your Github page. Submit the link to your final version to me through slack chat.

Resume Project (150 pts) – Final Due Oct. 1 Oct. 6

  1. Design a first draft of your resume making conscious use of the basic design principles. To submit, send the file to me via slack chat before class. Due Sept. 22 (25 pts)
  2. After discussing some best practices when giving or receiving feedback, we will critique our classmates' resumes.
  3. Based on feedback, we will iterate on our designs, creating two more resume where we will further experiment with the principals of design discussed in class. These second versions are due in class Sept. 27th along with a one to two paragraph (4-6 sentences each) explaining the reason for the design changes and how they make use of the basic design principles. To submit, send the file to me via slack chat before class. Due Sept. 24th. (50 pts)
  4. Students will present their updated resume designs, discussing their thought process and how they applied the core design principals we've discussed for each iteration. They will solicit feedback from the class to understand what is working and where there are areas for improvement.
  5. We will run through a live coding demonstration to surface any hurdles you might face when developing your own resume.
  6. After settling on a single resume design, students will be responsible for building their own resumes with HTML & CSS. To submit, publish to github in in your portfolio folder. Submit the github link to me in slack chat.
    7. Coded Resume Requirements:
    1. The following basic HTML tags used appropriately: <!DOCTYPE html>, <html>, <head>, <span>, <div>, <title>, <body>, <p> (optional - if you don't have any paragraphs, you won't need this), <ol>, or <ul>, <li>, <a>, <img> (optional).
    2. The use of appropriate dividing tags including <header>,<main> and <section>. Depending on the layout of your resume, you may decide to use the <aside> tag as well.
    3. An external file sheet called labeled "style.css" containing your CSS. Note: do not include any inline HTML styles.
    4. Styles applied the content within each of the mandatory tags mentioned above. Styling related to the concept of the Box Model (e.g. padding, margin and border) around any element of your choice.
    5. Class names on at least three tags of your choosing, and CSS associated with those tags in your style.css file.
    6. Use of at least one typeface (no more than 3) and at least two font weights.
    7. Use of an accent color (nothing too crazy needed).
    8. Use of semantic CSS class names. If you need a refresher on what a semantic class name is, read this short article: https://css-tricks.com/semantic-class-names/
    9. Clear content hierarchy. Demonstrated understanding of basic design principles: proximity, repetition, contrast and alignment. In order words, are similar pieces of content styled the same way? Does the important information stand out? Is the content aligned in such a way that it's easy to read? Are similar groups of content chunked together in clear visual units?
    Make sure your code is saved in your portfolio folder. You should have a "resume.html" document as well as a separate stylesheet in your css folder named "resume-styles.css". Push these updates to your Github page. Submit the link to your final version to me through slack chat before class (not during!) on Oct. 1st otherwise late penalties will apply. :U

Portfolio (300 pts) – Final Due Dec. 1

Create a mobile-first portfolio that shows off work you have done and discussed the details of those projects.

Class critique and live demos will occur throughout this project.
  1. Find and share portfolios that are well designed and have content written/organized in compelling ways.
  2. Discussion of site goals and the importance of defining goals early on in any project.
  3. Group brainstorm session for content creation, followed by content grouping exercise.
  4. Create a sitemap for the portfolio to articulate the overall site structure and how you will navigate from page to page. (25 pts)
  5. With these design elements and content hierarchy in place, begin to sketch mobile, tablet and desktop size layouts for your portfolio site keeping in mind that these are not 3 separate experiences.(25 points)
  6. Document all text for your portfolio, including social profile urls, project details, headlines, tag lines, etc. We will share this content with the group. (25 pts)
  7. Evolve sketches into wireframes giving more definition to the layout, content and visual hierarchy. (50 pts)
  8. Document the styles you plan to use in a static styleguide document. (25 pts)
  9. Consider places to layer in simple animations to breathe a bit more life to the portfolio experience.
  10. Develop portfolio starting with mobile screen sizes adding in breakpoints to account for where the content needs to shift. Final deliverable (150pts)
In addition to the deliverables mentioned above, your coded portfolio must contain:
  1. Three distinct site sections (e.g. "About Me", "Contact", "Resume", Projects/Work" -- however you want to organize your content)
  2. Three examples of projects or work that you would like to display.
  3. Content tagged using appropriate HTML tags exhibiting best practices. Indention matters!

    4. Husky App Challenge (150 pts) – Due Date - TBD

    In the spirit of a Design Studio, we will concept, pitch, sketch, refine an idea for an UCONN related app. By the end of the class you will create a clickable prototype with your team and then pitch the idea/prototype to the class.

    Guidelines

    The app can be for:
    • Anything related to UCONN
    • A single or multiple tasks

    Here are some ideas to get you moving if you feel stuck.

    Bonus Points:
    • How can it make money?
      If in-app purchases are available, what are they and how to they enhance the experience
    • Plan for repeat engagement
      Consider how you might use push notifications or email marketing to keep customers coming back
    Process
    1. Diverge — Brainstorm ideas / app research & inspiration - 15 minute (individual exercise)
    2. Converge — Choose your best ideas (2 max) and write a quick pitch that you will give to your team to explain what the app is & does.- 10 minutes (individual exercise)
    3. Pitch idea(s) to team members - 20 minutes
    4. Team vote / refine idea - 10 minutes
    5. Diverge — Sketch the app screens - 10 minute (individual exercise)
    6. Converge — Present sketches to team for alignment - 15 minutes
    7. Refine sketches / wireframes & write a paragraph description for the app store describing what your product is / does
    8. Use the app Prototyping on Paper to stitch together the interaction through the app.
    9. Post the url to your clickable prototype to to slack including your paragraph description and the names of each of your team members.
    10. Present your app to the class, gathering feedback on what could be clearer, how the app could be more useful, how to keep customers coming back, etc.
